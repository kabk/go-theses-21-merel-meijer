Abstract

In my thesis I research how the narrative around looted cultural heritage objects gets created. I wanted to research this subject, because for Western societies these objects present the past, but for the countries where the object was looted from, this is still a part of their culture. With not changing the narrative of the object, the disbalance between the Western Society and the countries that were their former colonies will never change. I wanted to find out how the human centered approach influenced the narrative of a looted cultural heritage object, with in this case specific focus on the bust of Nefertiti. There are different parties involved, so by understanding them, I could figure out how the issue is structured. After writing my thesis, I can conclude that I still share the same values as the countries that are fighting for their right to have access to their cultural heritage object. In my thesis, I found out that the solution for approaching the issue around looted cultural heritage objects is communication. With this we can collectively start creating a new narrative around these object.
